### SURFACE-2026-05-16-01 — β4 (`clQ`) explicit-Euler instability above V_REF — h-stab drives runaway in ~7s at 0.4 throttle
- **Source:** feature:verify-auto (WP14.7 Phase 1, 2026-05-16)
- **Target level:** product:arch (mechanism revision — analogous to D14 surfacing β5; this surfaces β4 stability hole)
- **Type:** arch-gap / numerical-stability-bug / mechanism-design-bug
- **Priority:** high (blocks the new throttle-high parity fixture from asserting full-trajectory stability; same blocking shape as SURFACE-2026-05-12-03 for sustained-throttle Phase 2 missions; gates WP15 takeoff/landing and WP16 combat above V_REF)
- **Summary:** WP14.6 shipped the β4 fix in `aerosurface.ts:445-450` per the prior arch revision: `(ω × r)` is amplified by `(1 + clQ · max(v, V_REF) / V_REF)`, with the `max(v, V_REF)` floor preserving WP6.5's low-V β4 calibration bit-for-bit. The arch claim was that the floor "matches the V² growth of the destabilizing pitch moment from `incidenceRad`, keeping damping ratio constant." That claim is dimensionally correct in continuous time, but **falsifies at dt=1/60 once v exceeds V_REF=30**: the discrete-time damping stiffness exceeds what explicit Euler can integrate stably. WP14.7's new `throttle-high` parity fixture (0.4 throttle, 30s) is the first fixture that takes body |v| above V_REF for a sustained window and surfaced this immediately.
- **Diagnostic data (captured via /tmp/diagnose-blowup.ts, 2026-05-16):**
  - **Ticks 0–407:** low-throttle phugoid oscillation, bounded. Body |v| < V_REF=30 throughout. β4's `vScale` is floored at 1; amplification = `(1 + 8·1) = 9` constant on h-stab.
  - **Tick 408:** body |v| crosses 30 m/s. h-stab sees |v|=59 m/s (vs body 11 — ω×r contribution dominates), force = 222,000 N.
  - **Tick 411:** body |v|=50, h-stab |v|=93, **force = 950,000 N**. h-stab force is now ~80× the wing force.
  - **Tick 412–414:** angvel sign-flips and grows: -2.4 → -25 → +38 → -262 rad/s. Classic stiff-damper signature: damper reverses ω each tick with more energy than ω had. Force: 21k → 2.6M → 10.9M N.
  - **Tick 415:** aircraft flips 180° pitch+roll+yaw in one tick. h-stab force = 1.27 × 10⁹ N.
  - **Tick 416:** |v| = 21,396 m/s. Force = 5.6 × 10¹⁷ N.
  - **Tick 417:** NaN/Infinity. All four surfaces, all components.
- **Surfaces involved:** h-stab is the primary driver (clQ=8, largest coefficient in `aircraft.json`). Wings (clQ=3) also contribute but are stable in isolation at this regime. v-stab (clQ=0) is unaffected.
- **Root cause:** numerical stiffness. `clQ=8` means the discrete-time effective damping coefficient on pitch rate, multiplied by lever arm² and dynamic pressure, exceeds `2/dt` once v > V_REF — putting the explicit-Euler-style update past its stability boundary. The "constant damping ratio" arch claim assumed continuous-time integration; at dt=1/60 the discrete-time system has a Nyquist constraint on coefficient magnitude that the formula does not respect.
- **Why we did not catch this earlier:** WP6.5's β4 calibration regime is `V < V_REF` (descending-glide attractor, where the floor keeps the formula at constant `(1 + clQ)`). WP10.5's β5 schema-landing parity test was 30s at mid throttle, also `V < V_REF` throughout. WP14.6's first parity fixture was mid throttle (same regime). No fixture exercised sustained `V > V_REF` until WP14.7 Phase 1.4 added throttle-high to close the `feedback_verify_self_envelope.md` gap. This is the lesson firing exactly as intended — envelope-widening surfaced a real bug, not a fake one.
- **Compounds with SURFACE-2026-05-12-03 (β5):** β5 was the original phugoid-damping mechanism that drove the D14 harness cascade. The harness was intended to search the β5 parameter space; we now have a *second* parameter (clQ) that also needs joint-search, and any β5 tuning is meaningless if β4 is unstable at the same operating point. The harness/optimizer cascade's search space is now (clQ, clAlphaDot) jointly, per surface — not clAlphaDot alone.
- **Suggested action (arch-revision candidate, parallel to D14's β5 treatment):**
  - **Option A — implicit-Euler form for the damping term:** apply the rotational amplification implicitly, so the discrete-time pole stays inside the unit circle regardless of clQ. This is the correct fix and matches how real flight-sim codes handle stiff aerodynamic damping. Requires its own WP.
  - **Option B — magnitude clamp on the amplified ω×r contribution:** clamp `|angCross_amplified| ≤ K · |angCross_unamplified|` for some K (say 5). Crude but preserves WP6.5 calibration when amplification is small and caps the runaway. Half-measure.
  - **Option C — clamp `vScale` itself:** cap `vScale` at some maximum (e.g. 3, equivalent to capping at v=3·V_REF=90 m/s). Smallest delta. Doesn't fix the underlying numerical-stiffness problem but raises the divergence threshold meaningfully.
  - **Option D — use the harness/optimizer (post-WP14.8) to search for a stable clQ at dt=1/60.** Lower clQ might be sufficient; the calibration came from real-aircraft data but our integrator's stability bounds matter more than the real damping derivative.
- **Verification approach when fixed:** WP14.7's `throttle-high` parity fixture is the canonical regression check. Full 1800-tick trajectory must stay finite, with `|browser - synthetic| < 1e-6` at every row.
- **Why we accept it for WP14.7 Phase 1 close:** The mechanism is pre-existing (shipped at the prior β4 revision, predating WP14.7). WP14.7's job is to build the harness, not to fix β4 — but the throttle-high fixture *will not* satisfy the original "full-trajectory parity" goal until β4 is fixed. The honest disposition: keep throttle-high in `PARITY_FIXTURES`, redefine the parity-diff assertion to "parity of divergence" (browser and synthetic must diverge at the same tick with the same NaN/Infinity pattern), document the SURFACE here, and move on. Phase 1 ships with a known mechanism gap surfaced and waiting for the harness to be able to *search* for a fix.

### SURFACE-2026-05-12-03 — β5 (`clAlphaDot`) mechanism diverges at any tuning value — needs arch revision (likely D14)
- **Source:** task:act (WP14.5 tuning attempts 1-3, 2026-05-12)
- **Target level:** product:arch (analogous to D13 → D14 mechanism revision; or WP10.5-style schema fix WP)
- **Type:** arch-gap / mechanism-design-bug
- **Priority:** high (blocks WP14.5 tuning side; blocks any sustained-throttle Phase 2 mission per SURFACE-2026-05-12-01; gates WP15 takeoff/landing and WP16 combat for non-trivial flight envelopes)
- **Summary:** WP14.5 attempted 3 tuning passes against the β5 mechanism at `src/aircraft/aerosurface.ts:475-483` (`cl += clAlphaDot · dα/dt` — raw rate, no V-normalization, no magnitude clamp). All three diverged catastrophically (alt to ±10³ to ±10¹² m, AS to 10⁵ to 10¹⁵ m/s within ~6s) at every tuning value tried:
  - **Attempt 1 (wings=+5, h-stab=+10):** SURFACE-2026-05-12-01's suggested starting range. ALL three throttle bands (0.05, 0.15, 0.4) diverged within 1-2s — not just the high-throttle target. The "no-op in working regime" goal from `feedback_asymmetric_fix_no_op.md` was violated catastrophically.
  - **Attempt 2 (wings=+1, h-stab=+2):** 5× smaller. Even worse — low/mid diverged in <2s; high diverged to 6.6e12 m alt, 8.4e14 m/s AS (vs pre-tune 6e10 m, 9e12 m/s — i.e. moving "with the wind" made divergence worse).
  - **Attempt 3 (wings=-1, h-stab=-2):** Tested the sign-convention hypothesis (sign-flipped tuning). Still diverges, but with reversed direction (alt → -26533 instead of +10⁹). Both signs blow up; the magnitude is the issue, not the sign alone.
- **Root cause (hypothesized):**
  1. The β5 mechanism uses **raw `dα/dt`** in rad/s without non-dimensionalization. At physics-tick scale (dt=1/60s), even 1° of AoA change per tick produces dα/dt ≈ 1.05 rad/s. Multiplied by clAlphaDot=1, that's a CL augmentation of 1.05 — comparable to the entire steady-state CL. Startup transients amplify this further (the aircraft settles into glide attitude in the first few ticks, producing large dα/dt).
  2. The mechanism creates a **positive-feedback loop** at certain sign/magnitude combinations: more lift → more pitch up → more α → more dα/dt → more lift.
  3. **Sign convention is documented but not physically validated.** The β5 unit test at `aerosurface.test.ts:1135` literally asserts that positive clAlphaDot + rising α produces additional lift — but additional lift on a rising α is **destabilizing**, not damping (analogous to the SURFACE-2026-05-10-01 AoA sign-convention bug lesson). The arch.md D13 description claims "positive clAlphaDot dampens" but does not derive this from physics.
- **Suggested action (arch-revision candidate D14):**
  - **Option A — non-dimensional form:** change the mechanism to `cl += clAlphaDot · (dα/dt) · c̄ / (2V)`, where c̄ is a reference chord length and V is body airspeed. This matches the standard aerodynamic form of cl_α̇ damping coefficients (Etkin/Reid). Adds a floor `max(V, V_REF)` analogous to WP6.6's β4 fix to avoid 1/V singularity at low V.
  - **Option B — magnitude clamp:** clamp `dα/dt` to [-DALPHA_MAX, DALPHA_MAX] (e.g., ±0.5 rad/s) before applying. Crude but kills the startup transient pathology.
  - **Option C — physics-side fix:** revisit the sign convention. If the test at `aerosurface.test.ts:1135` is wrong about which sign damps, flip the sign in `aerosurface.ts:482` and update the assertion. Then re-test tuning with positive values.
- **Verification approach when fixed:** the ≥30s Playwright probe at `tests/e2e/phugoid-probe.spec.ts` is already in place (3 test missions in `public/missions/`); just remove the `test.skip(...)` line at the top of the file. The probe exercises throttle ∈ {0.05, 0.15, 0.4}. The probe also serves as the "regression: descending-glide must still work" check (low throttle = the WP14 waypoint-patrol regime).
- **Why we accept it for WP14.5 close:** Per `feedback_retune_attempt_budget.md`, three attempts refuted decisively. The mechanism is unsuitable for direct tuning. Continuing to tune would be wasted budget — the real fix is an arch-level revision of the mechanism (D13 → D14, analogous to how WP6.6 added V-scaling to β4 after WP6.5). WP14.5 closes as "tuning refuted; surfaced arch revision." The pre-WP14.5 baseline (clAlphaDot=0 everywhere) is unchanged — current behavior preserved.
