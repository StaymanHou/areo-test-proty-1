## Plan-time physics derivation (CLAUDE.md Rule #5)

Performed BEFORE re-reading arch.md D18 prose, per Rule #5's operator-as-architect-side honesty intent.

1. **Fuselage drag direction (right-hand-rule).** Drag opposes motion through still air. Airspeed = `−linvel`. Drag direction = `+v̂ · (−1) = −linvel.normalized()`. Magnitude = `q·A·CD_f` with `q = 0.5·ρ·V²`. Final: `F = −0.5·ρ·V²·A·CD_f · (linvel/V) = −0.5·ρ·V·A·CD_f · linvel` (one fewer division). Guard: `if V > epsilon` to avoid 0/0 at body rest. Applied at body origin → zero torque (matches arch.md: "fuselage drag is a pure translational damping term, not a rotational one").
2. **Induced-drag dimensional check.** Textbook polar: `CD = CD_0 + k·CL²`, k dimensionless. Augmentation `cd += inducedDragK · cl²`: cl dimensionless → cl² dimensionless → ΔCD dimensionless. ✓
3. **Numerical trace.** cl=0.5, k=0.15: ΔCD = 0.0375 (comparable to parasitic CD_min=0.02 at moderate AoA — textbook). cl=1.1 (near stall), k=0.15: ΔCD = 0.182 — induced drag dominates parasitic 9× near stall, matching textbook drag-rise. ✓
4. **Sign symmetry for cl².** Negative cl (inverted flight, cl=−0.5) → cl² = +0.25 → ΔCD positive. Induced drag is symmetric in lift sign — correct (real wings produce induced drag in both positive- and negative-G flight). ✓
5. **Augmentation point — pre or post β4/β5?** Textbook lifting-line: `CD_i = CL_actual²/(π·AR·e)` where CL_actual is the total lift coefficient including unsteady contributions. So induced drag should use the *post-β4/β5* CL (i.e. place the augmentation AFTER step 4b and 4c in `computeAeroForce`).

**Derivation matched arch.md D18 prose at every step.** No errata detected (contrast WP14.9b's SURFACE-2026-05-17-02 D17 cross-product errata). Informational plan-time prior — not a SURFACE.
