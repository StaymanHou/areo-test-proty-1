## Retrospect

- **What changed in our understanding:**
  - The β5 (`clAlphaDot`) mechanism implemented at WP10.5 is **mathematically dimensional-mismatched for the role it was meant to play.** It uses raw `dα/dt` in rad/s (~1-50 rad/s at startup transients) directly as a multiplier on a dimensionless lift coefficient. Standard aerodynamic `cl_α̇` damping uses the non-dimensional form `cl_α̇ · c̄ · α̇ / (2V)`, where `c̄/(2V)` is the small factor that scales raw rate down to a sensible coefficient delta. WP10.5 explicitly chose the simplified form (the comment at `aerosurface.ts:467-474` notes "distinct from the standard `cl_q·c̄/(2V)` form" — but flagged it as a *feature*, not a defect, on the assumption tuning would absorb the difference). It can't — the dimensional mismatch creates a divergence that no scalar can fix.
  - **The β5 unit-test sign assertion at `aerosurface.test.ts:1135` codifies the destabilizing sign as correct.** "Rising α with positive `clAlphaDot` produces additional lift" — but additional lift on a rising AoA at a positive-lift wing means more pitch-up → more α → more lift. That's positive feedback (amplification), not damping. This is the **same family of error** as SURFACE-2026-05-10-01 (the original AoA sign-convention bug): pure-math identity tests pass for a physically wrong sign because the math is internally consistent but doesn't anchor to physics. The codified lesson at the time was "physical-sign tests are the only reliable convention anchor" — and WP10.5 wrote a sign-convention test that asserted the wrong physics direction.
  - **Both prior open SURFACE items pointing at "phugoid undamped under throttle" were partial fixes built on a fundamentally broken mechanism.** The arch.md D13 decision (β5 schema, WP10.5 schema landing) felt like architectural close — but the mechanism was never actually validated against the divergent-phugoid behavior it was claimed to address. The schema shipping with default 0 made it look harmless; only this tuning task surfaced the underlying defect.

- **Assumptions that held:**
  - The bounded 2-3 attempt budget per `feedback_retune_attempt_budget.md` was correct discipline — by attempt 3 the failure mode was clearly mechanism-shaped, not parameter-shaped.
  - The dual-SURFACE risk-anchor from WP14 reflect (over-damping might break the just-shipped WP14 waypoint-patrol low-throttle regime) held with a vengeance — attempts 1 and 2 broke ALL three throttle bands, not just the failing one. The verify-self envelope discipline (`feedback_verify_self_envelope.md`) caught it on the first attempt rather than 5 ship-and-recheck cycles in.
  - Option (c) as an always-available decision was correct. Without it, the bounded budget would have been pressure to ship a worse hack.

- **Assumptions that were wrong:**
  - **Assumption: "The β5 mechanism is a tunable damping coefficient; we just need to find the right value range."** Wrong. The mechanism has a structural defect (no V-normalization, no magnitude clamp) that no parameter value rescues. SURFACE-2026-05-12-01's suggested starting range (wings 5-10, h-stab 10-15) was 1-2 orders of magnitude too large because the surface author was reasoning in dimensionless-coefficient space while the implementation operates in raw-rate space.
  - **Assumption: "Architectural side of phugoid is closed at WP10.5; just need to tune."** Wrong. The arch close at WP10.5 landed a schema and a mechanism, but the mechanism was never validated against the divergent-phugoid behavior. "Schema landed with parity (Vitest green)" was treated as "arch decision validated" — but the parity gate (default 0 preserves prior behavior) does nothing to verify that non-zero values produce damping.
  - **Assumption: "If two attempts fail, sign-flip is a reasonable next try."** Half-right. Sign-flip was useful diagnostically (proved both signs blow up) but wasn't going to fix the mechanism. With hindsight, attempt 3 should have been "sign-flip with magnitude even smaller (e.g., -0.05)" to confirm whether smallness could rescue the working regime without rescuing the failing regime — that would have been a stronger refutation. As executed, the sign-flip at the same magnitude only proved sign-invariance of the failure mode.
