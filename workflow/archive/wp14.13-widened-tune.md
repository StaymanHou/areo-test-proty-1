## Plan-time physics derivation (CLAUDE.md Rule #5 — performed BEFORE re-reading arch.md D19 prose)

1. **Energy balance check under widened D19 bounds.** At the WP14.12 globalBest (`k_wing=0.436, k_hstab=0.125, cd0=0.617, area=2.410`), deployed terminal velocity in unpowered descent at cl=0.5 was: drag-area = `(0.02 + 0.436·0.25)·13.5 + 0.617·2.410 = 1.74 + 1.49 = 3.23 m²` → `V_t = √(2·9810/(1.225·3.23)) = 70 m/s`. But observed peak AS was 313 m/s at throttle-low. The disconnect is the **transient** vs **terminal** — phugoid dives don't equilibrate within 1800 ticks at this T_phugoid. Widening bounds to k_wing=1.5: at level cl=0.5, drag-area becomes `(0.02 + 1.5·0.25)·13.5 + 1.0·area ≈ 5.27 + area`. With area widened to 10, total ≈ 15.3 m² → `V_t ≈ 32 m/s` at level CL=0.5. **More importantly**, induced drag scales with `cl²`, so during dive recovery (high cl), induced drag dominates: at cl=1.5 (heavy g-pull during recovery), `ΔCD_i = 1.5·2.25 = 3.375`, drag-area ≈ 45+ m² instantaneously. This is the *transient* dissipation that bounds the phugoid amplitude.
2. **Dimensional sanity.** `inducedDragK · cl²` is dimensionless (k dimensionless, cl dimensionless, ΔCD dimensionless). ✓. `fuselageDrag.cd0 · area · 0.5·ρ·V²` has units `[1] · [m²] · [kg/m³] · [m²/s²] = kg·m/s² = N`. ✓.
3. **Sign check.** Induced drag is added (never subtracted): `cd += k·cl²` with `k>0`. ✓. Fuselage drag opposes linvel: `F = −0.5·ρ·V²·area·cd0 · v̂` with `cd0>0, area>0, ρ>0`. ✓. No sign-inversion possible at the widened bounds.
4. **Numerical trace at k_wing=1.5 (the new upper bound).** Level cruise at cl=0.5: ΔCD_i = 0.375 per wing → ~5× baseline CD per wing → total wing drag-area ~2.5× baseline. At T=6000 N max thrust, level cruise V solves `T = drag` → `6000 = 0.5·1.225·V²·(0.395·13.5) = 3.27·V²` → V = 43 m/s. **Risk: at k_wing=1.5, level cruise is slow** — feel may deteriorate. Mitigation: the throttle-mid score regime directly penalizes feel-deterioration in level flight; the optimizer will avoid the upper bound if it hurts the score function.
5. **Confidence:** P(criterion 1 PASS) ≈ 92% (more drag doesn't introduce new NaN paths; D18 mechanism well-behaved at finite values). P(criterion 2 PASS | criterion 1 PASS) ≈ 55% (3× headroom in two bounds; the gap is 309,000× but the optimizer is at-bound on those two knobs). **Joint P(Branch A) ≈ 50%.** P(Branch B) ≈ 50% with subsequent ranking per arch.md D19 table.

## Retrospect

- **What changed in our understanding:** The asymmetric/symmetric methodology mismatch went from a *calibration nuisance* (WP14.11/14.12 — known but stable: deploy was just systematically worse than search by a constant factor) to a *binding methodological gap* (WP14.13 — the direction of the asymmetric/deploy ratio INVERTED, meaning the optimizer's gradient now points away from flyable deployments). This shifts the architect-layer diagnostic axis: prior architect cycles (D17/D16/D18/D19) asked "which mechanism layer to add"; the next cycle asks "how to align the optimizer's search-airframe with the deployed-airframe before any further mechanism work can produce reliable signal." The mechanism: heavy `(clQ, clAlphaDot, inducedDragK)` applied to BOTH wings synchronously creates a coupled symmetric pitch-axis instability that the asymmetric airframe (one wing at baseline) avoids by breaking symmetry. This is *not* what `feedback_tune_cli_search_vs_deploy.md` originally warned about — that warning was about the magnitude of the gap, not its direction.

The optimizer's gradient followed the asymmetric-airframe contours, not the deployed-airframe contours, because the score function only sees the asymmetric airframe.
