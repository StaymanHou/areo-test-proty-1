## Retrospect

- **What changed in our understanding:** Three things. (1) The optimizer's `score` does NOT reflect the deployed-symmetric-airframe — it's evaluated over an asymmetric airframe where `surfaces.1` keeps `aircraft.json` baseline. This was invisible at plan time because the WBS canonical command was authored before this was understood. (2) `finite-doesn't-mean-flyable`: harness CSVs say "no NaN," but airspeed peaks of 230-373 m/s and altitude swings of 70-100m are unplayable. The score-function envelope (ALT/AS/PR/PHU) was doing its job — catching this exactly. (3) D17 + D16 together open a real finite-region in the joint (clQ, clAlphaDot) space, but the flyability bottleneck isn't β4/β5 mechanism shape anymore — it's something else (drag-CD, inertia tensor, or unsteady-aero). The WBS WP14.11 contingency anticipated this; the surprise was that "all 3 regimes finite" did NOT imply "flyable."
- **Assumptions that held:** D17+D16 mechanism implementations are correct (Vitest 525/525 preserved across the WP; numerical finiteness achieved in a non-trivial region of the parameter space — partial-success per SURFACE-2026-05-23-01). The score function's multi-regime envelope correctly distinguishes "no NaN" from "flyable feel" — that envelope is a working tool, not a tuning bug. The Phase 1 re-probe correctly predicted the β4 stable region under D16 would remain `[0..~1.5]` per surface (since β5 is dormant at clAlphaDot=0).
- **Assumptions that were wrong:** (1) The canonical WBS command's bounds `[0..15]^4` were too wide — the optimizer's 4 restarts couldn't find the lower-clQ basin where stable points actually live (restart sampling distributed poorly in 4D over `[0..15]^4`). SURFACE-2026-05-17-03's recommended `[0..2]` per clQ would have been better. (2) The plan assumed "if cross-threshold then ship" — but the threshold's interpretation was ambiguous about whether the optimizer's reported score IS the deployed score. It's not. Plans for tune-pass WPs should add an explicit "score the deployed-config (mirrored) airframe via score-deployed.mjs" verification step. (3) The plan's branch-A vs branch-B framing assumed a binary outcome; the actual outcome was tertiary: numerical-finite-region exists (partial success) but no flyable point in the searched space.
- **Approach delta:** Plan called for 4 phases; ran all 4 (Phase 3 skipped). Added 3 sub-tasks to Phase 2 (P2.4-P2.6: narrow-bounds re-run, deployed-config score, branch decision) discovered during the run — these were judgment calls under Mode 4 to avoid a back-loop-to-plan when the additional work was a natural follow-up to the canonical command's failure. Wrote `tools/tune/score-deployed.mjs` as a new permanent tooling utility that didn't exist at plan time — surfaced by the asymmetric-eval discovery. Did NOT modify the harness or tune CLI (out of scope), but flagged the `--mirror` flag as a tooling fix candidate in SURFACE-2026-05-23-01.
