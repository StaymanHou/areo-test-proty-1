## T1 — Mechanism formulas (populated 2026-05-24)

All per-surface forces are applied via `body.addForceAtPoint(_forceBuf, _pointBuf, true)` at the world-frame application point of the surface. Fuselage drag is applied via `body.addForce(_forceBuf, true)` at the body origin (no torque). Per-surface contributions in `computeAeroForce` (`src/aircraft/physics-core/aerosurface.ts:527`):

1. **Base lift:** `F_lift = q · A · cl · normal_world` where `q = 0.5 · ρ · v²` and `normal_world` = `surface.normal` rotated by body quaternion. CL is looked up from the curve at the local-frame α.
2. **Base drag:** `F_drag = q · A · cd · airflow_world / |airflow_world|`. Drag points along +airflow (which is `-(linvel + ω×r)`).
3. **β4 D17 (clQ):** `cl += clQ · (ω · dampAxis_world) · c̄ / (2·max(V_body, V_REF))` — additive to CL; gated on `clQ ≠ 0`.
4. **β5 D16 (clAlphaDot):** `cl += clAlphaDot · ((α_now - α_prev)/dt) · c̄ / (2·max(V_body, V_REF))` — additive to CL; gated on `clAlphaDot ≠ 0` AND `dt > 0` AND `prevAoA ≠ undefined`.
5. **D18 induced drag (inducedDragK):** `cd += inducedDragK · cl²` — additive to CD using the β4/β5-augmented `cl`.

Fuselage drag in `flightmodel.applyForces` (`src/aircraft/physics-core/flightmodel.ts:158`):

6. **D18 fuselage drag:** `F_fuse = -linvel/|linvel| · q · area · cd0` applied at body origin (zero torque).

**Energy-injection candidates (cross-checked):**
- Base lift/drag and fuselage drag use the same world-frame velocity for direction → cannot inject net energy under the standard formula.
- β4 (clQ): adds CL ∝ ω. Sign convention per code comment is "damping" (anti-rotation moment). But sign correctness depends on `dampAxis` direction. **Suspect candidate** — if `dampAxis` direction is inverted under some surface orientation, β4 would ADD energy to rotation instead of dissipating.
- β5 (clAlphaDot): adds CL ∝ dα/dt. Sign convention per comment is "positive clAlphaDot adds lift on rising α, which damps phugoid." If sign is inverted, β5 amplifies oscillation. **Suspect candidate.**
- D18 induced drag: ΔCD = k·cl². cl² is always positive, k ≥ 0 enforced at parse. Drag direction = +airflow which always opposes motion (perpendicular to airflow). **Cannot inject energy by formula** — but interaction with augmented `cl` from β4/β5 could compound.

**Hypothesis to test at T2/T3:** the energy-injecting mechanism is either β4 (clQ amplification of angular motion → wing lift growth → more angular motion via h-stab AoA → ...) or β5 (clAlphaDot amplification of dα/dt → wing lift growth → α increases → ...). The induced-drag coupling makes both worse because larger augmented cl → larger cl² → more drag — but drag is dissipative, so D18 alone shouldn't inject.

**Decisive finding: the energy injection is NOT from any D17/D16/D18 mechanism.**

- Zeroing β4 alone changes injection by ~1% (the mechanism is roughly neutral).
- Zeroing β5 alone REDUCES injection by 10% (β5 is *mildly damping* — its sign is correct).
- Zeroing either D18 drag term INCREASES injection (drag is dissipative — removing it leaves more energy in the body).
- Zeroing ALL D17/D16/D18 mechanisms together produces the WORST injection: 3.95 MJ over 60 ticks at AS=93.8 m/s, pitch=+24.5°.

The injection is present in pure pre-cascade base aerodynamics: q·A·CL along world-rotated normal + q·A·CD along airflow direction. **The cascade's mechanism additions (β4 D17, β5 D16, D18 drag polar) have been net-damping but unable to overcome a much larger upstream injection.** They were the wrong layer of intervention — they tried to damp instabilities, not eliminate the energy source.

This matches the existing CLAUDE.md note about pre-WP14.5 baseline behavior: "Pre-WP14.5 red baseline (clAlphaDot=0 everywhere): low/mid throttle bounded, high (0.4) diverges at t≈6s." The high-regime divergence has been there since WP9.6 — under the same fixed base lift/drag implementation. Eight architect revisions (D14→D23) added mechanisms attempting to damp it, none addressed the source.

**Energy budget arithmetic (sanity check):** at tick 30, BASELINE shows ΔE/tick ≈ 46.5 kJ ≈ 2.8 MW with available thrust power ≈ 110 kW. **The aircraft is generating ~25× its available thrust power as mechanical energy.** This is physically impossible — the only sources of energy are thrust work and gravity work (which is already accounted for via PE change). Therefore the base lift/drag formula is doing positive net work on the body in a regime where it should not.

**Hypothesis (preliminary, to be confirmed by D24 investigation/architecture cycle):** the lift direction `surface.normal.applyQuaternion(bodyState.quaternion)` is taken as the world-frame surface normal of the BODY-FRAME wing. This is NOT the same as "perpendicular to the freestream airflow direction" (which is the textbook definition of lift direction). At α≠0, body-frame normal has a component along the freestream direction → lift does net work along motion → energy injection. The correct formulation is: lift direction = airflow_world × (chord_world × airflow_world), normalized — i.e. perpendicular to airflow AND in the plane defined by airflow + chord. This is the "wind axes" vs "body axes" distinction.

Real aircraft simulators use either: (a) wind-axes decomposition (lift ⊥ airflow, drag ‖ airflow) with CL/CD looked up by α; OR (b) panel-method body-frame integration where each surface contributes pressure normal to its panel. The current code mixes both: it computes α relative to chord (good — α is well-defined), but applies lift along the body normal (wrong — should be perpendicular to airflow, not perpendicular to the chord).

## T4 — Root cause identified (populated 2026-05-24)

**Bug:** `body.resetForces()` and `body.resetTorques()` are never called between physics ticks. Rapier's `addForce` / `addForceAtPoint` accumulate into a persistent buffer that is NOT cleared by `world.step()`. Each tick re-applies all previous ticks' forces in addition to the current tick's, so the effective force grows linearly with tick count.

**Evidence:**

1. **Isolated probe with aero zeroed (surfaces.0..3.area = 1e-6) at throttle 0.4:** Δvz per tick observed:
   - Tick 0: -0.040 m/s ✓ (matches thrust 2400 / mass 1000 / 60 Hz)
   - Tick 1: -0.080 m/s (2× expected)
   - Tick 2: -0.120 m/s (3× expected)
   - Tick 10: -0.440 m/s (11× expected)
   - Tick 55: -2.240 m/s (56× expected)
   - **Pattern:** Δvz at tick n = -(n+1) · 0.040. The thrust force is applied (n+1) times at tick n.

2. **Same probe at throttle=0:** Δvz per tick = 0 (correct, vz stays at -30); vY = -8.83 m/s at tick 54 (correct gravity-only free-fall).

3. **Fix verified:** adding `aircraft.body.resetForces(true); aircraft.body.resetTorques(true);` before `fm.applyForces(...)` at the start of each tick produces correct Δvz = -0.040 per tick at every tick. See `tools/tune/probe-fix-test.mjs`.

4. **Test author was aware of the contract:** comment at `src/aircraft/physics-core/flightmodel.test.ts:411` states "Rapier exposes `body.userForce()` to read the accumulated user-applied force this tick; `resetForces` clears it before world.step." But the production path (`src/aircraft/physics-core/step.ts:33-35`) does not call resetForces. Only `rigidbody-core.ts:81-82` (in `reset()`, used only at trajectory-start) does.

**Buggy code site:** `src/aircraft/physics-core/step.ts:32-36`:
```typescript
export function step(world, _aircraft, flightModel, inputs, dt): void {
  flightModel.applyForces(inputs.throttle, dt);  // adds forces to accumulator
  world.timestep = dt;
  world.step();                                  // integrates — but does NOT clear accumulator
}
```

**Fix:** Add a `aircraft.body.resetForces(true); aircraft.body.resetTorques(true);` call at the start of `step()` (or equivalently before `flightModel.applyForces()` inside applyForces itself). The same fix must be applied to the browser game loop in `src/main.ts` (or wherever the browser-side equivalent of `step()` is called per tick) — without it the browser observes the same compounding behavior.

**Impact assessment — how does this explain the cascade?**

This bug has been present since at least WP9.6 (the cascade's beginning). Every "destabilizing motion" the cascade has been trying to damp via β4 (D17), β5 (D16), drag polar (D18), etc. has been driven by the underlying force-accumulation runaway. The cascade's mechanism additions were architecturally honest but applied to the wrong layer — they were trying to dissipate energy that an integrator bug was injecting.

Quantitative back-check: WP14.5 noted "Pre-WP14.5 red baseline (clAlphaDot=0 everywhere): low/mid throttle bounded, high (0.4) diverges at t≈6s." With the force-accumulation bug, at t=6s = 360 ticks, the effective thrust would be ~360× = ~864 kN on a 1000 kg airframe = ~864 m/s² forward acceleration. That alone explains the t≈6s divergence in high regime. Low and mid regime stayed bounded longer (thrust 300 N / 900 N respectively → effective ~108 kN / 324 kN at t=6s, still extreme but at lower threshold of integrator stability before NaN).

**Why this wasn't caught earlier:**

1. **Trim spawn (WP6.5) and early WPs spawned the aircraft at low AS with low throttle**, so the absolute force magnitudes were small even when compounded over 60+ ticks. The bug grew with throttle × time, and Phase 1 testing was mostly short-duration low-throttle.
2. **The parity test (WP14.7)** compares the harness to the browser — but the BROWSER has the same bug, so they agree (compound at the same rate). Parity holds bit-for-bit while both paths are broken in the same direction.
3. **Unit tests with `body.userForce()` snapshot single-tick force values** (e.g. `flightmodel.test.ts:430+` for D18 fuselage drag) and ran ONE applyForces call — so they tested the correct first-tick force value but never observed the cumulative integration over many ticks.
4. **The Vitest harness `step.test.ts` uses short trajectories** for unit-level integration tests; the bug's effect over 60+ ticks at non-trivial throttle was only observable in the full harness/optimizer runs — which were interpreted as "the airframe needs more damping" rather than "the integrator is broken."
5. **The optimizer's c0-floor cluster** that the cascade kept hitting is exactly what a compounding-force integrator produces: NaN-saturated trajectories from runaway forces. Each tune cycle's "globalBest" found parameter regions that minimized the runaway *briefly* before it exploded — exactly the c0-floor signature.

**This is a one-line fix.** Adding two lines to `src/aircraft/physics-core/step.ts` would resolve the force-accumulation runaway. The downstream impact is enormous: the cascade's 8 architect cycles (D14, D17, D16, D18, D19, D20, D21, D22, D23), 6 substantive tune cycles, and ~13 days of work were all chasing a symptom of this bug. Many of the additions may still be valid (β4 pitch damping is a real aerodynamic phenomenon), but the architectural urgency was misframed.
