# Feature: WP14.14b — D21 score-function revision

**Workflow:** feature

## Problem Statement

The harness score function (`tools/tune/score.ts`) has been measuring deviation from `targetAirspeed: {low:25, mid:30, high:40}` since WP14.8. The D21 architect cycle (arch.md Revision 2026-05-24 evening) derived that the current airframe's L=W equilibrium AS is `{low:~45, mid:~60, high:~85}` — ~2× higher than the score function's targets across all three regimes. The 22,000× harness-score gap (deployed −6.7M vs threshold −300) that drove the D14/D17/D18/D19/D20 cascade was dominated by envelope-against-wrong-target mismatch, not by an unfixable physics gap. Independent evidence: a post-WP14.14-finalize in-browser inspection (not committed; reverted) revealed that even the cascade's empirical best CANNOT sustain 1 second of level flight at the phugoid-probe-mid throttle 0.15 entry conditions (pitch −73°, AS 16.5 m/s, nose-first dive at t≈1s) — a "structural unflyable" pattern that all numerical proxies (NaN-tick, envelope-deviation) had missed for the entire cascade.

D21 picks **score-function revision singularly** (per `feedback_surface_or_means_or.md`; rejects option-c reframed-as-descending-glide and fifth-mechanism candidates). The revision adds criterion 0 "level-flight-maintenance" probe (1-second window from spawn; fail if altitude drops > 20m OR airspeed < 10 m/s; NaN-floor with prefer-failing-later encoding) AND re-calibrates per-regime `targetAirspeed` defaults to physically-derived L=W equilibrium AND tightens `AS_ENVELOPE` from 30 to 25. WP14.14b is the tooling-only implementation: `tools/tune/score.ts` schema + impl + Vitest. Downstream WP14.15 runs the canonical 8-dim linked-search tune at WP14.13 widened bounds under the new score function and is when the revised score function gets exercised against the physics layer.

**This WP does NOT touch `aircraft.json`. It does NOT touch any physics-mechanism code under `src/aircraft/`. Pure tooling.**

CLAUDE.md Rules #1/#2/#4 do NOT bind (no physics-mechanism schema field added). Rule #5 fired at D21 architect-cycle stage and is already discharged (the L=W equilibrium derivation is in arch.md D21 Revision body). Rule #3 does NOT bind (no `aircraft.json` edit). Rules #7/#8 are newly authored by D21 and are meta-rules about diagnostic-infrastructure discipline — they apply prospectively to future tune-deploy WPs (binding gates at WP14.15+), not to this WP itself.

## Retrospect

- **What changed in our understanding:** The "penalty squared" wording in arch.md D14.4 is slightly misleading — `score.ts:159-164` squares each individual penalty term (`asPenalty * asPenalty`) and sums them, NOT squares the sum. My first verify-self test case (e) assumed `(penalty²)²` = `10000` for an asPenalty of 10, but the actual computation is `10² = 100`. Caught at first Vitest run; fixed in one test-only edit. Cross-impact in `tune.test.ts:makeCleanCsv` also caught at the full-suite run — that helper hardcoded `airspeed=30` and now needed a per-throttle mapping under the new defaults. Both were minor and caught by the test gate, not by manual review.
- **Assumptions that held:**
  - Score function is a pure-compute change; no aircraft.json edit needed at this WP (matched arch.md D21 split).
  - The criterion 0 logic is straightforward TDD; the schema-shape addendum (3 new fields) was the right level of abstraction.
  - Vitest's existing test conventions (`describe` per case, helper functions for synthetic trajectories) extended cleanly to criterion 0 cases.
  - The cross-impact in `tune.test.ts` was the ONLY external consumer test that needed updates (score-deployed.mjs is a script, not a Vitest target, so no test-side change there).
  - P(WP14.14b Branch A) ≈ 95% at architect-cycle prediction → actually 100% (clean ship); the prediction was well-calibrated.
- **Assumptions that were wrong:**
  - I initially expected ~50 LOC new in score.ts; actual was ~60 LOC (the `LevelFlightCheckResult` discriminated union added a few lines; the file-header doc expansion was longer than estimated). Still well within the size S envelope (≤200 LOC).
