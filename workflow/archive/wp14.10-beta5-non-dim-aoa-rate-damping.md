# Feature: WP14.10 — β5 non-dimensional form (D16) for AoA-rate damping

## Problem Statement

The β5 mechanism (`clAlphaDot` AoA-rate damping) currently uses a raw-rate form at `src/aircraft/physics-core/aerosurface.ts:591-592`: `cl += surface.clAlphaDot * dAlphaDt`. Per SURFACE-2026-05-16-04 evidence, this is dimensionally wrong at physics-tick scale — `clAlphaDot=0.1` (the smallest tested value) NaN's within 85-199 ticks because the augmentation is comparable to steady-state CL during typical startup transients. arch.md D16 (Revision 2026-05-16) prescribes the textbook non-dimensional form `cl += clAlphaDot · dα/dt · c̄ / (2 · max(V, V_REF))` — Etkin & Reid §5.10–5.12, structurally parallel to D17's β4 treatment. The `chordLength` cache and `BETA4_V_REF = 30` constant are already live (landed via WP14.9 commit `9ebaf47`, reused by WP14.9b). D16 only changes the formula on one line. The default-zero parity contract holds by construction (`0 · anything = 0`); the WP10.5 sign-convention tests at `aerosurface.test.ts:1302/1321` continue to pass because `c̄/(2V) > 0` preserves sign. Per CLAUDE.md Rule #1, the WP must observe live behavior at a non-zero `clAlphaDot` in ≥2 regimes before any sign-test is trusted, and per Rule #2 + Rule #4, verify-self requires a baseline + non-default + control (clAlphaDot=0) triple gate against the SURFACE-2026-05-12-03 + SURFACE-2026-05-16-04 evidence. `aircraft.json` is UNCHANGED in this WP (still `clAlphaDot=0` on all surfaces); the retune is WP14.11's job under the WP14.10/WP14.11 two-close-gates split that worked at WP14.9b.

## Plan-time physics derivation (CLAUDE.md Rule #5)

Performed BEFORE reading arch.md D16 prose; comparison confirmed match.

1. **Sign.** `clAlphaDot · dα/dt · c̄/(2V)`. Multiplication by positive scalar `c̄/(2V)` preserves the pre-D16 sign relationship (validated at WP10.5: positive clAlphaDot × rising α → +ΔCL → damps phugoid). ✓
2. **Dimensional.** `c̄` [m], `2V` [m/s] → `c̄/(2V)` [s]. Times `dα/dt` [rad/s] = dimensionless. Times dimensionless `clAlphaDot` = dimensionless ΔCL. ✓ (Etkin & Reid reduced-frequency form.)
3. **Numerical trace.** At `c̄=1, V=30, dα/dt=1, clAlphaDot=1`: ΔCL = `1 · 1 · 1 / (2·30)` = 1/60 ≈ 0.01667. Matches WBS sanity-test target.
4. **vs raw form.** Same inputs, raw form gave ΔCL = 1 (60× larger). At typical startup transients (dα/dt ~ 10 rad/s), raw form gave ΔCL ~ 10 (catastrophic); non-dim gives ~0.167 (tunable).
5. **vs arch.md D16.** Matches exactly. No errata detected in D16 prose (unlike SURFACE-2026-05-17-02 for D17's cross-product order).

## Retrospect

- **What changed in our understanding:** Two regimes were needed for Rule #1, and the plan-time choice of "throttle-low + throttle-high" was infeasible under the current `aircraft.json` because the baseline β4 setup (clQ=3,3,8,0) NaN's at throttle-high — independent of β5. The two-regime requirement was satisfied by substituting throttle-mid for throttle-high, which keeps the test clean by isolating the β5 mechanism from the β4 narrow-stable-region problem. The substitution preserves arch.md D16's intent ("≥2 regimes, low-V + high-V OR rising-α + falling-α") and the larger throttle-high regime is owned by WP14.11's joint optimizer. **Lesson: when verifying a single mechanism in a multi-mechanism context, regime selection must isolate the variable under test.** The session-pause sanity check already anticipated this ("the baseline NaN's at throttle-high is a WP14.11 problem"), but the WP plan still listed low+high — caught at verify-self execution time, recorded as a documented deviation rather than a back-loop.
