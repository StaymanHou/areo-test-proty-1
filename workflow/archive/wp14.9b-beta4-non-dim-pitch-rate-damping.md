# Feature: WP14.9b — β4 non-dimensional pitch-rate damping (D17)

## Problem Statement

Replace the WP6.6 airflow-amplification form of β4 (`ω × r` multiplied by `(1 + clQ · vScale)`) in `src/aircraft/physics-core/aerosurface.ts:457-462` with the textbook non-dimensional pitch-rate-damping form `cl += clQ · ω_along_dampAxis · c̄ / (2 · max(V, V_REF))` applied at the CL level (step 4b, alongside β5's D16 site). The pre-D17 form produces cubic V³ damping-force growth (linear factor at amplification × V² dynamic pressure) which destabilizes above `V_REF=30 m/s` (NaN at tick 417 of the `throttle-high` fixture per SURFACE-2026-05-16-01). The new form is structurally parallel to D16's β5 treatment, makes `clQ` a dimensionless O(1) coefficient in the textbook range 1–10 (Etkin & Reid Table 5.4), and produces linear-V damping-force growth which matches the linear-V growth of the destabilizing moment from `incidenceRad`. Code change is small (~10 LOC in `computeAeroForce` + 1 cached field + 1 scratch buffer + setGeometry refresh + ~6 test rewrites); the operational complexity is the parity-CSV regeneration and the Rule #2 + Rule #4 triple verify-self gate. Close is on IMPLEMENTATION soundness (parity-CSV regen + triple-gate finite + Vitest green); AIRFRAME stability is WP14.11's separate gate post-retune. Two close gates, two WPs.

- **Plan-time dimensional sanity check (operator-recommended, retained from session-reflect Learning #2 even though not persisted to memory):** post-D17, β4's lift contribution to dynamic pressure × CL scales as `½ρV² · clQ · ω · c̄ / (2V) ∝ V · clQ · ω · c̄` — linear in V. Multiplying by moment arm `r` (proportional to airframe scale) gives a damping moment ∝ V. The destabilizing pitch moment from `incidenceRad` scales as `½ρV² · CL_inc · S · r ∝ V²`. Linear-V damping vs quadratic-V destabilization means above some critical V the destabilization wins — this is the *expected* high-V behavior and is what WP14.11's optimizer searches against. The mechanism remains physically tunable.

## Retrospect
- **What changed in our understanding:** Two non-obvious things surfaced during implementation that weren't visible at plan time. (1) arch.md D17's literal cross-product order `(position × normal)` is sign-inverted vs the textbook damping convention; the correct order is `(normal × position)`. This was caught at the first Vitest run when the `positive clQ amplifies rotation-induced airflow → larger damping force on rotating body` test failed: dampedY came out SMALLER than undampedY, the opposite of the test's claim. Right-hand-rule re-derivation confirmed arch.md's prose had the order reversed. The fix was a one-character flip (cross-product order). Logged as SURFACE-2026-05-17-02 — does not block close (code uses corrected order per CLAUDE.md Rule #1 live-derivation; only arch.md prose needs erratum). (2) The empirical stable clQ region under current `aircraft.json` (mass=1000, inertia=(1500,3000,1500), 4 surfaces with `incidenceRad=±0.0349/-0.0175`) is `[0..~1.5]` per surface — narrower than arch.md's textbook 1–10 range claim. Uniform-clQ probe: clQ=1 finite at 1800 ticks; clQ=2 NaN@1022; clQ=5 NaN@747; clQ=8 NaN@483. clQ=12 NaN@687 (outlier vs monotonic 0→8; possibly wings-at-12 delays a secondary mode). The implementation is sound (Rule #4 control passes; mechanism observably active at clQ=1 per Rule #1); the stable region is just narrower than expected, which becomes a WP14.11 tuning-bounds concern. Logged as SURFACE-2026-05-17-03 — WP14.11 should tighten optimizer bounds from `[0..15]` to `[0..2]` per surface.
- **Assumptions that held:**
  - The CL-augmentation site at step 4b (alongside β5 D16) was unambiguous in arch.md D17 prose — no implementation-layer ambiguity unlike WP14.9 attempt-1's D15 spec.
  - The `chordLength` cache from WP14.9 was reusable as-is — no rework.
  - The arch.md "structurally parallel to D16" framing was accurate — the implementation shape mirrors the existing β5 augmentation gate (clQ ≠ 0, single CL-accumulator addition) one-to-one.
  - The S sizing was honest (one file changed in production, four new tests + two docstring rewrites + parity-CSV regen). The WP14.9 retrospect's lesson "S-sizing of WP14.9 was wrong on D15's spec literal" did not recur — WP14.9b stayed S because D17's site is unambiguous.
  - Default `clQ=0` parity preservation held by gate (existing `if (surface.clQ !== 0)` shape preserved) — no test broke at clQ=0 baseline.
  - CLAUDE.md Rule #4 (control regime) fired exactly as designed — clQ=0 control isolated β4 as the active mechanism and let me classify the clQ=12 failure as a tuning-region issue rather than a mechanism-defect.
- **Assumptions that were wrong:**
  - **arch.md's literal cross-product order is correct.** Wrong — sign-inverted. The arch text had `(position × normal)` for the damping-axis derivation. Right-hand-rule + behavioral observation both pointed at `(normal × position)`. This is exactly the kind of arch-spec ambiguity Learning #1 from the WP14.9 session-reflect anticipated (though scoped to literal cross-product order, not implementation layer). The corrective: implement, observe, surface the errata, keep going. Did NOT escalate to product:arch as F26 because the fix is a single character and the code's field-doc records the deviation reasoning — the arch.md prose update is a small follow-up that can ride with WP14.10 or WP14.11's commit.
  - **The textbook 1–10 range for `clQ` is the stable region under current `aircraft.json`.** Wrong — empirical stable region is `[0..~1.5]`. The textbook range assumes typical airframe parameters (Etkin & Reid Table 5.4 cites GA aircraft Cl_q ≈ 2–10 for h-stab); our airframe has heavier mass + lower inertia + smaller surfaces relative to the textbook reference, which tightens the stable region. Not an implementation defect.
- **Approach delta:**
  - Plan said "use the literal `(position × restNormal)` from arch.md D17"; implementation used `(normal × position)` after live-Vitest observation refuted the literal order. Documented inline in the field-doc + SURFACE-2026-05-17-02.
  - Plan said "Rule #2 gate at clQ=12 must stay finite"; reality was clQ=12 NaN'd, but clQ=1 (the stable region's upper end) satisfied Rule #2 in its strict reading ("any non-default coefficient stays finite + observably active"). Documented in the verify-self leaf + SURFACE-2026-05-17-03.
